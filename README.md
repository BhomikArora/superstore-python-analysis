# Superstore Sales Analysis — Python

Exploratory data analysis of the Superstore sales dataset using 
Python, Pandas, Seaborn and Matplotlib to extract actionable 
business insights across 9,994 orders spanning 2014–2017.

## What This Project Covers

- **Regional Performance** — identifying top and underperforming markets
- **Root Cause Analysis** — 3-factor investigation into why South underperforms
- **Category & Margin Analysis** — why Furniture has high sales but low profit
- **Furniture Deep Dive** — sub-category profit and discount investigation
- **Monthly Sales Trend** — growth patterns and seasonal peaks (2014–2017)
- **Customer Segmentation** — Consumer, Corporate and Home Office analysis
- **Discount Impact Analysis** — how discounting is hurting profitability
- **Pareto Analysis** — top 10% of customers generate 30% of revenue
- **RFM Segmentation** — 793 customers segmented into 6 behavioral groups

## Key Findings

- West leads sales ($725K), South underperforms due to low footfall 
  and poor retention — not pricing
- Tables and Bookcases operate at a loss due to discounts above 20%
- 175 Champion customers average $4,708 spend at 8.8x purchase frequency
- 215 At Risk customers represent ~$500K in recoverable revenue
- Orders with discounts above 20% consistently result in losses

## Tools Used
Python, Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Matplotlib, Jupyter Notebook

## Dataset
Sample Superstore | 9,994 rows | 22 columns | 2014–2017
